Metformin inhibits hepatic gluconeogenesis by activating AMPK and is the first-line treatment for type 2 diabetes.
Beta-blockers competitively antagonize catecholamines at beta-adrenergic receptors, reducing heart rate and contractility.
Warfarin inhibits vitamin K epoxide reductase, reducing synthesis of clotting factors II, VII, IX, and X.
ACE inhibitors block the conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II, reducing vasoconstriction and aldosterone secretion.
Statins competitively inhibit HMG-CoA reductase, the rate-limiting enzyme in cholesterol biosynthesis.
Morphine binds to mu-opioid receptors in the central nervous system to produce analgesia.
Aspirin irreversibly acetylates cyclooxygenase, inhibiting thromboxane A2 synthesis and platelet aggregation.
Digoxin inhibits the Na+/K+-ATPase pump, increasing intracellular calcium and enhancing cardiac contractility.
Aminoglycosides bind the 30S ribosomal subunit and inhibit bacterial protein synthesis.
Penicillins inhibit transpeptidase, preventing cross-linking of peptidoglycan in bacterial cell walls.
Benzodiazepines potentiate GABA-A receptor activity by increasing the frequency of chloride channel opening.
Loop diuretics inhibit the Na-K-2Cl cotransporter in the thick ascending limb of the loop of Henle.
Proton pump inhibitors irreversibly block the H+/K+-ATPase in gastric parietal cells.
Fluoroquinolones inhibit DNA gyrase and topoisomerase IV, preventing bacterial DNA replication.
Heparin potentiates antithrombin III, inactivating thrombin and factor Xa to prevent clot formation.
Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors block the serotonin transporter, increasing synaptic serotonin.
Calcium channel blockers reduce calcium influx into vascular smooth muscle and cardiac cells, causing vasodilation.
Thiopurines are prodrugs that are converted to 6-thioguanine nucleotides, inhibiting purine synthesis.
Tamoxifen is a selective estrogen receptor modulator that competitively inhibits estrogen in breast tissue.
Allopurinol inhibits xanthine oxidase, reducing the production of uric acid in gout management.
